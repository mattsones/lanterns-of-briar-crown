# Chapter 1 Environment Prompts

Use these prompts as starting points for future environment generation. They should be adjusted based on selected art.

## Master Style Block

```text
Storybook fantasy illustration with a hand-drawn, hand-painted feel, subtle painterly texture, soft brushwork, gentle ink-like line definition, and a warm illustrated-book atmosphere. The image should feel handmade rather than slick or overtly digital. Use a middle-ground character style: expressive and warm, like storybook villagers and adventurers, but not overly cartoony or exaggerated. The world should feel whimsical, adventurous, and emotionally sincere, with room for gentle humor. Use a clean fantasy visual language with soft parchment-like warmth in the palette and composition. Lighting should be cinematic but painterly, with cozy glow where appropriate.
```

## Global Negative Prompt

```text
No photorealism, no 3D-rendered look, no glossy digital game art, no anime style, no harsh comic-book ink, no horror tone, no grimdark mood, no over-saturated neon colors, no overly detailed clutter, no exaggerated caricature proportions, no modern clothing, no sci-fi elements, no copyright-specific franchise resemblance.
```

# Chapter 1 Key Art

```text
Create a storybook fantasy key art illustration for an original adventure game. Show a youthful village adventurer at the beginning of a journey, standing on Lantern Road. The hero should feel sincere, brave, and still early in the adventure, wearing simple village travel clothes, a short cloak, practical boots, a belt pouch, and carrying a modest starter weapon such as an old hatchet.

In the distance, show Hearthhollow as a small, warm, cozy village with glowing cottage windows and inviting lantern light. Farther along the road, the world should feel more magical and uncertain. Lantern Road should appear ancient, benevolent, and enchanted, with subtle old route markings, lantern motifs, or sacred roadside signs that suggest the road preserves truth and guidance.

Somewhere in the image, include a subtle sign of the Briar Crown: a rough, invasive, thorn-like false mark or scratched symbol imposed over older truthful road signs. It should feel like an intrusion, not the main focus.

The emotional tone should be adventurous, warm, slightly urgent, and gently whimsical. The image should feel like the cover illustration of a family-friendly fantasy novel or storybook adventure game, with a sense of real stakes but no darkness or horror.

Use the master style block.
```

# Hearthhollow Gameplay Map

```text
Create a painted storybook gameplay map of Hearthhollow, a cozy fantasy village in a forest clearing. The map should be readable as a playable area with tile-based movement underneath, but visually feel like a hand-painted board-game/storybook map.

Include a central well, warm cottages, garden plots, soft lantern light, rounded paths, simple fences, a smithy, a small potion shed, a home cottage, an elder’s cottage or gathering place, a supply chest area, and a south gate exit. The south gate should look like a friendly village gate and actual road exit, not a separate danger location. Do not put a boar sign over the gate.

The mood should be warm and cozy with slight tension beyond the gate. Use golden light, warm browns, soft greens, parchment highlights, and subtle painterly texture. Avoid embedded text labels; icons and labels can be added later as UI overlays.

Use the master style block.
```

# Lantern Road Gameplay Map

```text
Create a painted storybook gameplay map of Lantern Road, an ancient enchanted road through a forest. The map should feel playable with clear paths and distinct interactable locations, but still hand-painted and atmospheric.

Include a winding old road, an old Lantern Shrine, a milestone ruin, a broken cart, a traveler camp, a roadside cache, an ambush bend, and exits toward Hearthhollow and Bramblecross. The road should have subtle route marks and old lantern symbols worked into the stones, but avoid too many glowing lanterns.

The road should feel clearly magical, benevolent, and a little sacred. Add one rough, invasive Briar Crown mark scrawled over an older true-road symbol. The contrast should be clear: old marks guide and protect; the false mark commands and corrupts.

Use muted greens, amber road light, mossy stone, dusky blue shadows, and subtle painterly texture.

Use the master style block.
```

# Bramblecross Town Map

```text
Create a painted storybook gameplay map of Bramblecross, a practical and guarded fantasy town. The town should contrast with Hearthhollow by feeling more square, structured, civic, and watchful.

Include town walls, gates, a prominent watchhouse, market area, inn, notice board, organized streets, sturdy stone and timber buildings, posted papers, and civic banners. The architecture should feel practical rather than cozy: more straight lines, stone grays, brown timbers, muted reds, and parchment tones.

The town should feel tense but not oppressive. It is trustworthy, organized, and worried. The map should be readable for tile-based or node-based movement underneath.

Use the master style block.
```

# Root Cellar Gameplay Map

```text
Create a painted storybook dungeon map of the old Root Cellar beneath Bramblecross. The cellar should feel ancient, earthy, mysterious, and tense but not horror-focused.

Include readable connected chambers and paths, roots threading through stone, old storage rooms, lantern-lit shadows, a root sigil, an old route mural, a glowcap mushroom cluster, a cellar cache, and a sealed iron door. A Briar Knot Warden should clearly block the approach to the sealed door so the player cannot reach the door without passing the guardian.

Use earthy greens, browns, muted golds, warm amber lantern light, mossy textures, and subtle painterly detail. The layout should feel playable and readable, not a chaotic cave.

Use the master style block.
```
